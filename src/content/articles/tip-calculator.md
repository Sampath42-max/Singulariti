In many countries, particularly the United States and Canada, tipping is not merely a gesture of goodwill; it is a vital component of a service worker's income. Waitstaff, bartenders, hairdressers, and drivers often rely on gratuities to make a living wage. 

Therefore, calculating a tip accurately is both a matter of social etiquette and financial fairness. 
Conversely, nobody wants to accidentally over-tip significantly due to a math error, especially on a large bill. Using a Tip Calculator ensures that you are compensating the service professional fairly without putting an unnecessary dent in your own wallet.

Using a Tip Calculator is designed to be the most intuitive experience possible. Here is the standard workflow:

### E-E-A-T and Tipping Etiquette: Pre-Tax or Post-Tax?

A common area of confusion—and a mark of an authoritative tip calculator—is the debate between tipping on the pre-tax or post-tax amount. 

According to traditional etiquette experts, you are only obligated to tip on the *pre-tax* amount of the bill, as a tip is a reward for the service provided, not a percentage of the state's sales tax. However, in modern practice, many people simply tip on the final post-tax total for the sake of simplicity. A high-quality, trustworthy tip calculator will provide you with the option to choose, ensuring you are calculating exactly according to your personal preference and budget.

### Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

**What is the standard tip percentage in a restaurant?**
In the United States, the standard tipping range is between 15% to 20% for sit-down restaurant service. 15% usually denotes average service, 18% is for good service, and 20% or more is reserved for excellent service. 

**How do I use the Tip Calculator for food delivery?**
For food delivery (like pizza or app-based delivery services), a standard tip is typically 15% to 20% of the bill, or a flat rate of $3 to $5, depending on the size of the order and the distance traveled. The calculator can easily help you find the 15% mark on a large family order.

**Should I tip on alcohol?**
Yes, you should include the cost of alcohol when calculating your tip at a restaurant. If you are sitting at a bar, the standard rule of thumb is $1 to $2 per drink, or 20% of your total bar tab.

**Does a Tip Calculator work with different currencies?**
Yes. Because a tip calculator operates on percentages, it works perfectly regardless of the currency. Whether you are entering US Dollars, Euros, or Japanese Yen, 20% is mathematically the same. 

**Is it rude to use my phone to calculate the tip at the table?**
Not at all! In fact, it is entirely commonplace and expected. Waitstaff prefer that you use a calculator to ensure their gratuity is calculated accurately rather than guessing and accidentally shortchanging them.
